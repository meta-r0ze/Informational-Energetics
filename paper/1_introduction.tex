\section{Introduction}
The structures of the observable universe, from biological cells to the quantum vacuum, share a defining property: they \textit{persist} against the thermodynamic current that erases distinction. Control theory describes how systems regulate against fluctuations, but treats the regulation target (setpoint) as arbitrary. Information theory quantifies the cost of information processing, but treats the processor as given. Thermodynamics establishes the arrow of time, but treats entropy as a property of \emph{states}, not of \emph{processing}.

To model persistence, in \cref{sec:IE} we synthesize \textbf{Informational Energetics} (IE): the study of how systems convert energy into structural information to resist entropic decay. This yields the \textbf{Universal Architecture of Persistence}: six existential constraints (Capacity, Map, Protocol, Governor, Toll, Margin) that define the lifetime of any persistent entity. This paper validates IE through its most demanding application, fundamental physics, but the framework applies to any persistent system.

Standard models of particle physics treat the dimensions, symmetries, and constants of the vacuum as empirically measured parameters. Here, we derive these same quantities as emergent, optimal solutions to a systemic control problem problem aimed at maintaining structural coherence with zero external energy flux.

\subsection{Information Theoretical Context of Physics}

The concept that physical reality is fundamentally information processing is rooted in the work of Wheeler (``It from Bit'') \cite{wheeler_information_1989} and Landauer \cite{landauer_irreversibility_1961}. More recently, Verlinde\cite{verlinde_origin_2011} proposed that gravity is an entropic phenomenon emerging from information gradients and Bianconi\cite{bianconi_thermodynamics_2025} proposed that gravity arises from quantum entropy coupling matter with geometry. While concordant with Landauer, IE applies this logic broadly to all persistent systems, treating the minimization of Entropic Action as the primary driver of their dynamics. All derivations follow from applying these principles.

\subsection{Testing the Framework: Deriving the Vacuum}
The vacuum possessing zero free parameters and no external environment is the ideal test case for IE. We apply IE to physics in three strict derivation layers, where each builds only upon the previous, with no appeal to physical phenomena not yet derived.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{System 0: Requirements.} We map the six pillars to physics, deriving Finiteness, Unitarity, and Causality as existential necessities (\Cref{sec:System0_vacuum_requirements}).
    \item \textbf{System 1: Substrate.} System 0's requirements uniquely specify an $E_8$ lattice projected to $D=4$ spacetime, yielding the characteristic integers $\mathbb{S} = \{\Delta=43, \nu=16, \sigma=5, \chi=2\}$ (\Cref{sec:system1_projection}).
    \item \textbf{System 2: Impedance.} We derive the fine-structure constant ($\alpha^{-1}$) as a parameter-free geometric consequence of this substrate (\Cref{sec:system2_geometric_impedance}).
\end{enumerate}

To distinguish this from numerical combination approaches, we stress that $\alpha^{-1}$ is derived through a strict deductive hierarchy. $\alpha^{-1}$ is constrained by IE, the substrate geometry, and the characteristic integers. With zero free parameters, the result is a rigid structural prediction; any alternative invariant would shift the result beyond experimental bounds, falsifying the framework. This paper validates the cascade through the first Standard Model parameter $\alpha^{-1}$.

\subsubsection{The \texorpdfstring{$E_8$}{E8} Lattice: Substrate vs. Algebra}
The exceptional Lie group $E_8$ has long been explored as a candidate for unification due to its status as the smallest symmetry group large enough to host the Standard Model. Most famously, Garrett Lisi proposed embedding the Standard Model directly into the $E_8$ algebra \cite{lisi_exceptionally_2007}. However, the \textbf{Distler-Garibaldi theorem} \cite{distler_there_2010} established a severe no-go condition that any signature-preserving algebraic embedding of the Standard Model into $E_8$ cannot reproduce chiral fermions without introducing unobserved mirror fermions. This theorem is widely considered the primary roadblock to $E_8$ unification.

The framwork circumvents the conditions of the no-go theorem because the theorem applies strictly to signature-preserving algebraic embeddings. We treat $E_8$ as the \textbf{Geometric Substrate} (the fundamental hardware) and not as the Gauge Algebra (the effective field). By projecting the Euclidean $E_8$ lattice $(8,0)$ onto a Lorentzian spacetime manifold $(1,3)$, the required metric signature change dynamically suppresses the mirror sector. As detailed in \cref{app:geometric_origin_of_chiral_fermions}, chirality emerges strictly from the geometric projection as a kinematic consequence of the metric signature change, rather than an algebraic input requiring mirror cancellation.

\subsection{Distinction From Physics-First Approaches}
Several research programs explore discrete spacetime structures (Causal Set Theory \cite{bombelli_space-time_1987}, Loop Quantum Gravity \cite{rovelli_quantum_2004}) or emergent gravity (Verlinde \cite{verlinde_origin_2011}, Jacobson \cite{jacobson_thermodynamics_1995}). While many research approaches treat spacetime or gravity as fundamental starting points, this work explores a \textit{systems-first} methodology. We derive universal persistence requirements from Informational Energetics and evaluate candidate substrates. We demonstrate that the $E_8$ lattice naturally satisfies these constraints, emerging as the optimal geometric candidate rather than an \textit{a priori} assumption. This yields predictions that physics-first approaches do not address, while simultaneously \textbf{providing a structural rationale for \emph{why}} the Standard Model has its specific configuration it does (gauge groups, generation count, chirality, etc.). This does not replace the Standard Model; rather, it provides the structural duality, the informational 'hardware' that necessitates the Standard Model's specific 'software' configuration.
