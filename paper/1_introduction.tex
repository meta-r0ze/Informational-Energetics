\section{Introduction}
The structures of the observable universe, from biological cells to the quantum vacuum, share a defining property: they \textit{persist} against the thermodynamic current that erases distinction. Control theory describes how systems regulate against fluctuations, while treating the regulation target (setpoint) as an externally specified boundary condition. Information theory quantifies the cost of information processing, but treats the processor as given. Thermodynamics establishes the arrow of time, but treats entropy as a property of \emph{states}, not of \emph{processing}.

To model persistence, in \cref{sec:IE} we synthesize \textbf{Informational Energetics} (IE): the study of how systems convert energy into structural information to resist entropic decay. This yields the \textbf{Universal Architecture of Persistence}: six existential constraints (Capacity, Map, Protocol, Governor, Toll, Margin) that define the lifetime of any persistent entity. This paper validates IE through its most demanding application, fundamental physics, but the framework applies to any persistent system.

Standard models of particle physics treat the dimensions, symmetries, and constants of the vacuum as empirically measured parameters. Here, we derive these same quantities as emergent, optimal solutions to a systemic control problem aimed at maintaining structural coherence with zero external energy flux.

\subsection{Information Theoretical Context of Physics}

The concept that physical reality is fundamentally constrained by information processing costs is rooted in Landauer's principle \cite{landauer_irreversibility_1961} and discussed by Wheeler (``It from Bit'')\cite{wheeler_information_1989}. More recently, Verlinde\cite{verlinde_origin_2011} proposed that gravity is an entropic phenomenon emerging from information gradients and Bianconi\cite{bianconi_thermodynamics_2025} proposed that gravity arises from quantum entropy coupling matter with geometry. While concordant with Landauer, IE applies this logic broadly to all persistent systems, treating the minimization of Entropic Action as the primary driver of their dynamics. All derivations follow from applying this principle.

\subsection{Testing the Framework: Deriving the Vacuum}
The vacuum possessing zero free parameters and no external environment is the ideal test case for IE. We apply IE in three strict derivation layers, where each builds only upon the previous, with no appeal to phenomena not yet derived.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{System 0: Requirements.} We map the six pillars to physics, deriving Finiteness, Unitarity, and Causality as existential necessities (\Cref{sec:System0_vacuum_requirements}).

We further identify a predicted \textit{Quantization Gap} between kinematic and high-loop QED extractions, consistent with the substrate's finite channel capacity, and propose a falsification criterion.
\end{enumerate}

We stress that $\alpha^{-1}$ is constrained through an eliminative filter: IE, the substrate geometry, and the characteristic integers jointly select a unique value with zero tuning parameters.

\subsubsection{The \texorpdfstring{$E_8$}{E8} Lattice: Substrate vs. Algebra}
The exceptional Lie group $E_8$ has long been explored as a candidate for unification because it is the smallest simple Lie group large enough to contain the Standard Model gauge group. Most famously, Garrett Lisi proposed embedding the Standard Model directly into the $E_8$ algebra \cite{lisi_exceptionally_2007}. However, the Distler-Garibaldi theorem \cite{distler_there_2010} established a rigorous no-go result: any signature-preserving algebraic embedding of the Standard Model into $E_8$ cannot yield chiral fermions without also producing unobserved mirror partners. This theorem remains the principal obstruction to $E_8$ \emph{algebraic} unification.

Here we do not employ $E_8$ as a gauge algebra (the effective symmetry of a quantum field theory). We instead treat the $E_8$ lattice as the \textbf{Geometric Substrate}, the fundamental discrete hardware on which effective dynamics emerge. 
This geometric projection dynamically assigns the Lorentzian signature $(1,3)$ to spacetime while the internal symmetry sector retains the original Euclidean signature $(4,0)$. Mirror states therefore reside in an internal space with no timelike generator and do not propagate on the observable manifold. As detailed in \cref{app:geometric_origin_of_chiral_fermions}, chirality emerges strictly from this geometric projection as a kinematic consequence, not as an algebraic input requiring mirror cancellation.

\subsection{Distinction From Physics-First Approaches}
Several research programs explore discrete spacetime structures (Causal Set Theory \cite{bombelli_space-time_1987}, Loop Quantum Gravity \cite{rovelli_quantum_2004}) or emergent gravity (Verlinde \cite{verlinde_origin_2011}, Jacobson \cite{jacobson_thermodynamics_1995}). While many research approaches treat spacetime or gravity as fundamental starting points, this work explores a \textit{systems-first} methodology (deriving physical structure from universal persistence requirements rather than postulating dynamical laws). We derive universal persistence requirements from Informational Energetics and evaluate candidate substrates. We demonstrate that the $E_8$ lattice emerges as the optimal geometric candidate rather than an \textit{a priori} assumption. This yields predictions that physics-first approaches do not address, while simultaneously establishing a structural rationale: the Standard Model's specific configuration (gauge groups, generation count, chirality, etc.) emerges as a consequence of persistence requirements rather than accepted as phenomenological input.
