\section{Algebraic Geometry of the Causal Loop and Complex Multiplication}
\label{app:algebraic_geometry_causal_loop}

This appendix provides the formal mathematical derivation bridging the physical requirement of Unitarity to the algebraic necessity of the Stark-Heegner numbers, as referenced in \cref{sec:fundamental_resonance_filter1}.

\subsection{The Setup: Dimensional Reduction to the Causal Plane}
While the substrate of the $E_8$-Persistence theory is an 8-dimensional geometric structure ($D_4 \oplus D_4$), information propagation is strictly localized. A fundamental state transition—a signal propagating from one node to another—defines a 1-dimensional spatial trajectory evolving over 1-dimensional time. This dimensional reduction restricts the act of causal propagation to a $(1+1)D$ \textbf{Causal Plane}.

Because the vacuum possesses a fundamental temporal cycle, this sequence of updates must eventually close, forming a periodic loop of length $\Delta$. 

\subsection{Mathematical Translation: The Elliptic Curve}
Topologically, a discrete, periodic $(1+1)D$ causal plane forms a 2-dimensional real torus ($\mathbb{T}^2$). Mathematically, this is equivalent to a 1-dimensional complex torus, known in algebraic geometry as an \textbf{Elliptic Curve}.

The geometry of this causal subtorus is governed by a complex modular parameter $\tau$. As established in \cref{sec:metric_signature}, time acts as the geometric imaginary unit ($i \equiv \omega$) due to the Lorentzian metric signature. 

Because this trajectory is physically constrained to the discrete, self-dual structure of the underlying lattice with rational coordinates, $\tau$ cannot be a generic continuous complex variable. Specifically, a 2-dimensional lattice with rational basis vectors has a period ratio $\tau = \omega_2/\omega_1$ that satisfies a degree-2 minimal polynomial over $\mathbb{Q}$. Since the definition of a non-degenerate torus requires $\text{Im}(\tau) > 0$, this mathematically forces $\tau$ into an imaginary quadratic field by definition—a standard result in the theory of elliptic curves with Complex Multiplication \cite{silverman_advanced_1994}. 

Consequently, the elliptic curve representing the causal loop possesses \textbf{Complex Multiplication (CM)}, with its endomorphism ring residing strictly within the imaginary quadratic field $\mathbb{Q}[\sqrt{-\Delta}]$.

\subsection{The Failure Mode: Isogeny Classes and Causal Degeneracy}
For the system to preserve quantum Unitarity, the history of a state traversing this causal loop must be uniquely reconstructable. Mathematically, this requires the endomorphism ring of the elliptic curve to be a Principal Ideal Domain (PID), which corresponds to a class number of $h=1$.

We analyze the failure mode where $h > 1$. In this regime, the ideal class group of the field is non-trivial. By the theory of Complex Multiplication, this dictates that multiple non-isomorphic elliptic curves will share the exact same endomorphism ring. 

Physically, these isogenous curves represent topologically distinct causal histories. However, because they are locally indistinguishable by their norm structure, a physical measurement at the output node cannot determine which of the $h$ inequivalent curves generated the state. 

\subsection{The Resolution: The Unitarity Constraint}
This local indistinguishability manifests as \textbf{Causal Degeneracy}. The causal history is fundamentally erased, not merely unknown. This ambiguity explicitly violates the unitary requirement that the evolution operator be uniquely invertible ($\hat{U}^\dagger \hat{U} = I$). 

To prevent effective information loss and preserve Unitarity, the universe must strictly forbid non-trivial ideal class groups in its fundamental causal cycles. The algebraic field defining the cycle must possess $h=1$, proving that the temporal period $\Delta$ is mathematically restricted to the Stark-Heegner numbers.
