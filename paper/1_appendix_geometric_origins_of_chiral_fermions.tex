\section{Geometric Origin of Chiral Fermions}
\label{app:geometric_origin_of_chiral_fermions}

Distler and Garibaldi \cite{distler_there_2010} demonstrated that a signature-preserving embedding of the Standard Model within $E_8$ cannot accommodate chiral fermions without unobserved mirror partners. Their theorem holds for all embeddings where the algebraic structure remains Euclidean $(8,0)$. We include this brief derivation for readers seeking explicit verification that the metric signature change $(8,0) \to (1,3) \oplus (4,0)$ evades the theorem by rendering mirror states dynamically inert.

The $E_8$-Persistence theory employs the symmetric decomposition $E_8 \to D_4 \oplus D_4$ (derived in \cref{sec:symmetric_decomposition}) coupled with the Metric Projection $(8,0) \to (1,3) \oplus (4,0)$ (derived in \cref{sec:metric_signature}), which falls outside this assumption. As established in \cref{sec:metric_signature}, the internal symmetry sector retains the original Euclidean signature $(4,0)$ and therefore lacks a timelike generator.

\paragraph{Formal Resolution:} The Distler-Garibaldi constraint assumes a \textit{signature-preserving} embedding. We explicitly violate this assumption. Rather than an algebraic homomorphism, we perform a geometric subspace restriction onto an observable Lorentzian spacetime manifold (e.g., Minkowski space $\mathbb{R}^{1,3}$). The orthogonal degrees of freedom, which contain the mirror states, reside strictly within the Euclidean internal sector. 

Because this Euclidean sector possesses signature $(+,+,+,+)$, its isometry group is the compact rotation group $SO(4)$. This group fundamentally lacks the timelike translation generator ($P_0 = \partial_t$) present in the spacetime Poincaré group $ISO(1,3)$ required for dynamical evolution.

Consequently, because the internal $SO(4)$ group and the spacetime Poincaré group commute, the mirror states $\psi_{\text{mirror}}$ possess no temporal Hamiltonian relative to the observable manifold. They satisfy the strictly stationary condition $\partial_t \psi_{\text{mirror}} = 0$. In an information-theoretic framework, a state without a time-evolution operator cannot propagate or encode a changing signal; it acts entirely as a static internal degree of freedom (a quantum number). Mirror fermions are therefore \textbf{geometrically suppressed}. They do not propagate on the observable $(1,3)$ manifold, evading the Distler-Garibaldi theorem by violating its implicit premise: that the full $E_8$ algebra acts dynamically in spacetime.
